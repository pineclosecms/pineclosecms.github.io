What is this?

This is a Content Management System (CRM) used specifically for logging incidents regarding the tenant of 14 pine close.

It’s organized into categories for 𝗮-𝘇 Assault, Audio, Document, Harassment, Main Incidents, photos, police, video, WDH and more that can be found listed on the far right.

How to navigate

Accessing Content: all logged incidents on this platform are readily accessible with a click.

Navigation Menu: Located at the upper right corner is a navigation bar. The ‘All’ button displays a comprehensive list of all logged incidents, which you can easily browse. Adjacent to the ‘All’ button, a dropdown arrow that provides a selection menu, allowing you to look at incident reports from any page without the need to return to the index.

Content Filters: Next to the dropdown, you’ll find icons representing different media types, which serve as filters:

  • 📽️ Video Camera = Video
  • 🔊 Audio Speaker = Audio
  • 🖼️ Picture = Photos
  • 📄 Document = Documents


Viewing Modes: Beneath these icons is a toolbar featuring a toggle switch to alternate between Dark and Light viewing modes.

Sidebar Features: On the far right, the sidebar presents several options:
Recent Posts: displays the latest incidents, sorted by date, accessible from anywhere.
Categories: Displays the volume of content logged under each category. Selecting a category reveals a curated list of content within that category.
Tags: Each logged incident is assigned one or more tags, indicating the type of content included, such as documents, videos, and photos. Tags are visually scaled based on the quantity of associated content; selecting a tag filters the view to show only incidents with that content type.
Calendar: Allows for filtering of content by month. Selecting a date on the calendar will update the display to show content from that specific time frame.

—-On October 11, 2024, at approximately 11:48 AM, law enforcement officers
—-made another attempt to apprehend the resident of 14 Pine Close.
—-This action followed numerous unsuccessful requests for the individual to
—-attend a voluntary appearance (VA). The resident has a documented history
—-of non-compliance with both law enforcement and housing authorities (WDH).
 1.  11:48 AM
—-Officers arrived at the location, as evidenced by photographic documentation.
 2.  11:50 AM
 —-At 11:50 AM (00:20 into the attached video), officers knocked loudly on the
—-door of 14 Pine Close. The resident ignored this attempt, despite being
—-audible inside the property shortly before and after the knocking.
 3.  Subsequent Attempts
◦    At 11:50 AM (00:42 in video), officers knocked again, followed by additional
—-knocks at 11:51 AM (01:10 and 01:44 in video).
◦    Shortly after, Lucy answered the door at 11:52 AM (02:55 in video) and spoke
—-with an officer for around 40 seconds before closing the door at 11:53 AM.
 4.  Continued Ignoring
◦    At 11:54 AM (04:08 in video), officers knocked on number 14 once more
—-but received no response.
◦    Between 11:55 AM and 11:56 AM (04:55 in video), officers were observed
—-walking around the area, looking up at the windows in an attempt to locate
—-the resident but were unsuccessful.
 5.  Further Attempts to Make Contact
◦    Officers knocked again at 11:58 AM (05:47 in video) and continued to be
—-ignored, with only an aggressive dog barking from within.
◦    Additional knocks occurred at 11:59 AM (06:10 in video) and again at 12:00 PM
—-(06:48 in video). A loud bang was heard from above during this time,
—-indicating the resident’s presence.
◦    Despite further attempts at knocking shortly thereafter
—-(12:00 PM, 07:02 in video), the officers were still ignored.
 6.  Revisiting Neighbor for Discussion
◦    At 12:02 PM (08:22 in video), officers returned to our door to discuss the
—-situation regarding number 14. During this time, they knocked again on
—-number 14 at 12:03 PM (08:46 in video) but received no response.
◦    Officers returned to speak with Lucy again at 12:03 PM (09:16 in video), where
—-they explained that due to the resident’s continued non-compliance and their
—-inability to confirm his presence, they could not enter the property.
—-They advised that we should call 101 when we are certain of his presence and

—-at 12:06 PM (11:01 in video).
◦    Additional evidence includes a photo of their arrival at 11:48 AM
